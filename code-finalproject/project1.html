User Story

Interviews

Goal
  • Understand the current system or structure of office hours in different universities
  • Whar are the issues, pain points and barriers undergraduate students face when attending office hours?
Approach

We interviewed approximately 15 students and 3 professors

Takeaways

Students said they discovered that most of their peers had the same doubts but nobody bought it up in class Professors desire a welcoming environment where students don't feel anxious or guilty about approaching them.

"I'm afraid I'll ask stupid questions and I get easily intimidated by professors who are not very approachable" ~Student


Secondary Research

Goal

We looked into several articles that gave us an insight into office hours and its structure, how it benefits the students and doesn't

Takeaways
We found that the two main aspects for encouraging students to attend office hours were social and logistical
The benefits that came from attending office hours were academic and relationship focused.

Testing

Goal
  • Are students able to navigate through the site to schedule office hours meetings?
  • What are students’ thoughts on the Forum, Student Profile, and Professor Profile
Approach

To evaluate how well students were able to accomplish the main objective of scheduling and to gather thoughts on other features of the site.

Takeaways
  • Path to scheduling is not clear, students are unsure where to start and require extensive guidance to schedule a meeting
  • scheduling conflicts are not obvious to the viewer
  • Confusion amount icons such as upvote, star and thumbs up
  • Anonymous posts need better differentation

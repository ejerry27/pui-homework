Interviews

Goal

To understand what is working, not working, and what could be improved/discontinued in the improved version.

Approach

I conducted around 8 interviews. This was a mix of students, SI leaders, and staff at the Academic Success Center. Supplemental instruction is one of the services provided by the Academic Success Center.

Takeaways
  • Finding a better way to organize the courses (group them by department or by chronological order)
  • Having a map on the schedule that shows the location
  • Being able to make it dark mode (accessibility)
  • University-wide holidays should be on the website
  • Follow Purdue branding or use less colors

“There is a lot going on at first glance, I get thrown off when it starts with Sunday but then there is no friday" ~Student

Testing

Goal

To understand if the prototypes made fulfill the shortcomings of the current website and see how it could be improved.

Approach

I tested my prototypes with SI leaders and students who attended SI sessions.

Takeaways

    For the table concept

  • Some of the students and the leaders thought that the table idea was too simple and looked like the current table that is on Brightspace. It looked boring to them and would not want it to be on the website
  • They also wished that there were more colors within the table that would help the students identify with the course better

    For the card style prototype

  • SI leaders seem to like this design better than the other one because it is aesthetically pleasing
  • They also mentioned that replicating the building changes for certain courses might be hard for this concept but it is possible to do it without changing the overall organization/layout
